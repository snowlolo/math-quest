Fractions
Parts of a whole, comparing, finding half

What is a fraction?

A fraction shows part of a whole. It is written as one number over another.

Top number (numerator) — how many parts we have
Bottom number (denominator) — how many equal parts in total

Example: 3/4 means 3 out of 4 equal parts.

Comparing unit fractions (top = 1)

When the top number is 1, a bigger bottom number means a smaller fraction. Think of cutting a pizza — more slices means each slice is smaller.

1/2 > 1/3 > 1/4 > 1/5 > 1/6 ...

Finding half of a number

1/2 of a number = divide by 2

Worked examples

Which is bigger: 1/4 or 1/7?
4 is smaller than 7, so 1/4 is bigger.
(Fewer slices = each slice is larger.)
Answer: 1/4
What is 1/2 of 18?
18 ÷ 2 = 9
Answer: 9
A shape has 6 equal parts and 4 are shaded. What fraction is shaded?
Shaded parts = 4 (top number)
Total parts = 6 (bottom number)
Answer: 4/6
×
Multiplication
Equal groups, skip counting, times tables

What is multiplication?

Multiplication means adding equal groups.

4 × 3 = four groups of 3 = 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12

How to work it out: skip counting

Say the first number out loud, that many times, counting by the second number.

6 × 4 - count by 4s, six times:
4 - 8 - 12 - 16 - 20 - 24

Worked examples

What is 7 × 3?
Count by 3s, seven times:
3 - 6 - 9 - 12 - 15 - 18 - 21
Answer: 21
What is 8 × 5?
Count by 5s, eight times:
5 - 10 - 15 - 20 - 25 - 30 - 35 - 40
Answer: 40
9 × ? = 63. What is the missing number?
Count by 9s until you reach 63:
9 - 18 - 27 - 36 - 45 - 54 - 63 (that's 7 steps)
Answer: 7
÷
Division
Equal sharing, opposite of multiplication

What is division?

Division means splitting into equal groups.

20 ÷ 4 = 5
"20 split into 4 equal groups = 5 in each group"

The shortcut: use multiplication

Division is the opposite of multiplication. If you know your times tables, you already know division.

If 6 × 7 = 42, then 42 ÷ 6 = 7 and 42 ÷ 7 = 6

Worked examples

What is 36 ÷ 4?
Ask: 4 × ? = 36
Count by 4s: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32, 36 - that's 9 steps
Answer: 9
What is 56 ÷ 7?
Ask: 7 × ? = 56
7 × 8 = 56 ✓
Answer: 8
30 students sit at tables of 6. How many tables?
30 ÷ 6 = ?
6 × 5 = 30 ✓
Answer: 5 tables

Place Value
Hundreds, tens, ones, and rounding

Every digit has a position

Example: 472

4 - Hundreds place - value is 400
7 - Tens place     - value is 70
2 - Ones place      - value is 2

400 + 70 + 2 = 472

Rounding to the nearest ten

Look at the ones digit.
5 or more - round up
Less than 5 - round down (keep the tens digit the same)

Worked examples

What is the value of the digit 5 in 357?
5 is in the tens place. Tens × 5 = 50.
Answer: 50
What number has 3 hundreds, 0 tens, and 8 ones?
300 + 0 + 8 = 308
Answer: 308
Round 74 to the nearest ten.
Ones digit is 4 - less than 5 - round down - keep tens digit (7) the same - 70
Answer: 70
Round 86 to the nearest ten.
Ones digit is 6 - 5 or more - round up - 90
Answer: 90

Telling Time
Reading clocks, quarter hours, elapsed time

The two hands

Short hand - the hour (where it points = the hour)
Long hand - the minutes

Where the long hand points

Long hand on 12 - :00 (on the hour)
Long hand on 3  - :15 (quarter past)
Long hand on 6  - :30 (half past)
Long hand on 9  - :45 (quarter to next hour)

Adding minutes

Count forward by 5s from the start time.

Worked examples

Short hand on 7, long hand on 6. What time is it?
Short hand = 7 (hour). Long hand on 6 = 30 minutes.
Answer: 7:30
It is 2:00. What time will it be in 45 minutes?
Count by 5s: 2:05 - 2:10 - 2:15 - 2:20 - 2:25 - 2:30 - 2:35 - 2:40 - 2:45
Answer: 2:45
A film starts at 3:00 and ends at 5:00. How long is it?
Count from 3 to 5: 3 - 4 - 5 = 2 hours
Answer: 2 hours

Measurement
Length, perimeter, area, mass, and capacity

Perimeter

Perimeter is the total distance all the way around a shape. Add up all the sides.

Rectangle perimeter = length + width + length + width
= 2 × (length + width)

Square perimeter = side × 4

Area

Area is the space inside a shape. It is measured in square units (cm²).

Area of a rectangle = length × width

Worked examples

A rectangle is 8 cm long and 3 cm wide. What is the perimeter?
8 + 3 + 8 + 3 = 22 cm
(or 2 × (8 + 3) = 2 × 11 = 22)
Answer: 22 cm
A rectangle is 6 cm long and 4 cm wide. What is the area?
Area = 6 × 4 = 24
Answer: 24 cm²
A square has sides of 5 cm. What is its perimeter?
5 × 4 = 20
Answer: 20 cm

Mass (weight)

1 kg = 1000 g

To convert kg → g: multiply by 1000
Example: 3 kg = 3 × 1000 = 3000 g

Capacity (liquid volume)

1 L = 1000 mL

To convert L → mL: multiply by 1000
Example: 2 L = 2 × 1000 = 2000 mL

Answer: 22 cm

Word Problems
Finding the right operation from keywords

Step 1 - Look for key words

total / in all / altogether - ADD
left / fewer / difference - SUBTRACT
each / groups of / times - MULTIPLY
shared equally / split / per - DIVIDE

Step 2 - Write the number sentence

Put the numbers from the problem into a maths sentence, then solve.

Worked examples

Maya had 35 stickers and gave 12 to a friend. How many does she have left?
Key word: "left" - subtract
35 - 12 = 23
Answer: 23
There are 5 bags with 8 apples in each. How many apples in total?
Key word: "in each" and "total" - multiply
5 × 8 = 40
Answer: 40
24 books are shared equally between 6 students. How many does each student get?
Key word: "shared equally" - divide
24 ÷ 6 = 4
Answer: 4
=
The Equals Sign
Balance - both sides must be the same

The equals sign is a balance

The = sign means both sides of the equation must equal the same amount. Think of a weighing scale - it must be perfectly balanced.

5 + 3 = 4 + ?

Left side: 5 + 3 = 8
Right side must also = 8
4 + ? = 8 - ? = 4

How to solve it

Step 1: Work out the side you already know fully.
Step 2: The other side must equal the same number.
Step 3: Find the missing number.

Worked examples

6 + 7 = ___ + 5. What goes in the blank?
Left side: 6 + 7 = 13
Right side must = 13
___ + 5 = 13 - ___ = 8
Answer: 8
___ + 4 = 3 + 9. What goes in the blank?
Right side: 3 + 9 = 12
Left side must = 12
___ + 4 = 12 - ___ = 8
Answer: 8
→
Patterns
Finding the rule, extending sequences

What is a pattern?

A number pattern is a sequence of numbers that follow a rule. The rule tells you how to get from one number to the next.

Finding the rule

Step 1: Subtract each number from the one after it.
Step 2: If the difference is the same each time, that's your rule.

Example: 3, 6, 9, 12, ...
6 − 3 = 3, 9 − 6 = 3, 12 − 9 = 3
Rule: + 3 each time

Counting down

The rule can also subtract each time.
Example: 40, 35, 30, 25, ...
Rule: − 5 each time — next term = 20

Worked examples

What comes next: 5, 10, 15, 20, ___?
Difference = +5 each time.
Answer: 25
Fill in the missing number: 8, 12, ___, 20, 24
Difference = +4. The missing term is 12 + 4 = 16.
Answer: 16
What comes next: 50, 45, 40, 35, ___?
Difference = −5 each time. 35 − 5 = 30.
Answer: 30

Data
Bar graphs, mode, mean, and median

Reading bar graphs

A bar graph uses bars to show quantities. Read the height of each bar against the number scale to find the value.

To find the most popular / most votes: look for the tallest bar (highest number).

Mode

The mode is the number that appears most often in a data set.

Example: 3, 5, 3, 7, 3, 5
3 appears 3 times — Mode = 3

Mean (average)

Step 1: Add all the numbers together.
Step 2: Divide by how many numbers there are.

Example: 4, 6, 8, 10
Sum = 28 — Divide by 4 — Mean = 7

Median

The median is the middle number when the data is in order.

Example: 2, 5, 8, 11, 14
Middle number = 8

Worked examples

Bar graph: Red = 8, Blue = 5, Green = 12, Yellow = 3. Which has the most?
Find the largest: 12 (Green).
Answer: Green
Find the mode of: 2, 4, 2, 6, 2, 4
2 appears 3 times, 4 appears 2 times, 6 appears 1 time.
Answer: 2
Find the mean of: 10, 20, 30, 40
Sum = 100 — Divide by 4 = 25
Answer: 25
Find the median of: 7, 3, 9, 1, 5
Put in order: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9 — middle = 5
Answer: 5
